# Main Discoveries Players Can Make

This document lists possible **high-level scientific discoveries** players can make about a procedurally generated star system and its planets.  
Most of these can be phrased as questions/contracts in-game (e.g. “Determine whether planet X has…”, “Explain why planet Y…”).

---

## 1. Star & System-Wide Discoveries

- What type of star is this (mass, temperature, age, metallicity)?
- How old is the star system?
- How massive was the original protoplanetary disk?
- Where was the original snow line located in this system?
- How many major planets formed in this system?
- How many dwarf planets and minor bodies exist in this system?
- Is this system dynamically stable over long timescales?
- Has the system undergone major rearrangements (migration, scattering)?
- Are there significant asteroid belts or debris fields?
- Are there comets or long-period bodies in the outer system?

---

## 2. Planet Formation & Evolution

- How did planet X form (in situ vs migrated)?
- Did planet X form beyond the snow line and migrate inward?
- Was planet X originally a gas-rich planet that lost its atmosphere?
- Did planet X grow through many small collisions or a few giant impacts?
- Did planet X experience a “late heavy bombardment”-like phase?
- Did interactions with gas giants shape the orbit of planet X?
- Has planet X’s orbit changed significantly over time (migration, circularization)?
- Was planet X once part of a larger body that was disrupted?
- Is planet X a captured object from another region of the system?
- Did tidal interactions with the star or other planets reshape planet X?

---

## 3. Orbital Architecture & Dynamics

- Why is planet X in its current orbit (semi-major axis, eccentricity, inclination)?
- Is planet X in orbital resonance with planet Y?
- Are planet X’s moons in resonance with each other?
- Is the orbit of planet X unusually eccentric or inclined?
- Are there signs of past close encounters between planets?
- Is the orbit of planet X currently stable or long-term chaotic?
- Has tidal locking occurred between planet X and the star?
- Are there Trojan bodies sharing an orbit with planet X?
- Is planet X part of a compact multi-planet chain?

---

## 4. Internal Structure & Geophysics

- What is the internal structure of planet X (core, mantle, crust, envelope)?
- How large is the metallic core of planet X?
- Does planet X have a differentiated interior (separated layers)?
- Does planet X have active tectonics?
- Is planet X volcanically active?
- Does planet X have cryovolcanism (ice volcanism)?
- What is the internal heat source of planet X (radioactivity, tidal heating, residual formation heat)?
- Does planet X have a subsurface ocean?
- How thick is the crust or ice shell of planet X?
- Has planet X’s interior partially or fully solidified?

---

## 5. Surface & Terrain

- What are the main surface types on planet X (rock, ice, ocean, desert, mixed)?
- Does planet X have large mountain ranges?
- Are there vast plains formed by lava flows or sedimentation?
- Is the surface heavily cratered or relatively young and smooth?
- Are there active or extinct volcanoes?
- Are there fault lines, rift valleys, or tectonic plate boundaries?
- Are there river channels, deltas, or erosion features?
- Are there dune fields or evidence of wind-driven processes?
- Are there glaciers or ice caps?
- Is there evidence of past oceans or lakes now dried up?

---

## 6. Atmosphere & Climate

- Does planet X have an atmosphere?
- How dense is the atmosphere of planet X?
- What is the atmospheric composition of planet X?
- Does planet X possess greenhouse gases that significantly affect temperature?
- Does planet X have clouds? If yes, what are they made of?
- How does temperature vary from equator to poles on planet X?
- How does temperature vary between day side and night side (especially if tidally locked)?
- Are there strong winds or jet streams on planet X?
- Are there storms, hurricanes, or long-lived atmospheric vortices?
- Has planet X lost a significant fraction of its atmosphere over time?
- Is atmospheric escape currently occurring on planet X?

---

## 7. Magnetism & Radiation

- Does planet X have a magnetic field?
- How strong is the magnetic field of planet X?
- What is the likely source of the magnetic field (dynamo in core, induced, none)?
- Does the magnetic field of planet X protect its atmosphere from stellar wind?
- Are there radiation belts around planet X?
- Are there auroras on planet X (detectable by instruments)?
- Has the lack of a magnetic field significantly eroded the atmosphere?

---

## 8. Water, Ice & Volatiles

- Does planet X have liquid water on the surface?
- Does planet X have liquid water below the surface (subsurface ocean)?
- Are there significant surface ice deposits on planet X?
- Are there polar caps made of water ice, CO₂ ice, or other volatiles?
- Are there underground ice reservoirs?
- Did planet X once host liquid water but lose it?
- Are volatiles (water, methane, ammonia, CO₂) trapped in clathrates or ices?
- Are there cryovolcanic plumes releasing water or other volatiles?

---

## 9. Rings & Moons

- Does planet X have a ring system?
- How did the rings around planet X form?
- What are the rings around planet X made of (ice, rock, dust, mixed)?
- Are the rings of planet X young or ancient?
- Are the rings stable or temporary?
- Does planet X have moons?
- How many moons orbit planet X?
- Are the moons regular (formed from the disk around planet X) or irregular (captured)?
- Did a destroyed moon or impact create the rings?
- Is there tidal heating in moon A caused by planet X?
- Does moon A have a subsurface ocean due to tidal heating?
- Are there active plumes or cryovolcanoes on moon A?

---

## 10. Chemistry & Surface Composition

- What is the bulk composition of planet X (rock, metal, ice, gas fractions)?
- What minerals dominate the surface of planet X?
- Are there metallic ores or rare elements detectable on planet X?
- Are there organic molecules on the surface or in the atmosphere?
- Is there complex organic chemistry occurring on planet X?
- Is there a global or regional distribution of certain minerals or ices?
- Are there chemically distinct regions (e.g. volcanic plains vs sedimentary basins)?
- Has the surface been chemically altered by atmospheric processes?

---

## 11. Atmospheric Chemistry & Biosignature Candidates

- Are there gases present that are commonly associated with life (e.g. oxygen, methane, nitrous compounds)?
- Can the observed methane on planet X be explained abiotically?
- Can the observed ammonia on planet X be explained abiotically?
- Is there a strong chemical disequilibrium in the atmosphere of planet X?
- Are there incompatible gases coexisting (e.g. high O₂ and high CH₄)?
- Are any “biosignature” gases varying seasonally?
- Are there photochemical hazes or aerosols in the atmosphere?
- Are isotope ratios suggestive of biological or purely abiotic processes?
- Is there any spectral evidence of surface pigments or unusual reflectance features?

---

## 12. Past and Present Habitability

- Has planet X ever been in the star’s habitable zone?
- Is planet X currently in the habitable zone?
- Did planet X’s climate remain stable long enough for life to potentially develop?
- Did planet X have surface liquid water for extended periods of time?
- Did planet X lose habitability due to runaway greenhouse or freeze-out?
- Are there protected niches (subsurface, deep ocean, under ice) that might be habitable?
- Is radiation at the surface of planet X too intense for life as we know it?

---

## 13. Life & Biosignatures (If Present / Suspected)

- Has there ever been life on planet X?
- Is there currently microbial life on planet X?
- Is there any evidence of complex life on planet X?
- Are there fossil or morphological biosignatures (e.g. stromatolite-like structures)?
- Are there chemical biomarkers in the atmosphere or surface samples?
- Are there isotopic signatures indicative of biological processing?
- Are any observed features better explained by life than by abiotic processes?
- Are there multiple independent lines of evidence pointing toward life?

---

## 14. Catastrophic & Historical Events

- Did a giant impact significantly alter planet X (e.g. strip atmosphere, tilt axis)?
- Did a collision create a large moon or ring system for planet X?
- Did planet X experience a global freezing or runaway greenhouse episode?
- Did planet X undergo a massive volcanic resurfacing event?
- Was a previous atmosphere of planet X lost catastrophically?
- Did nearby supernovae or stellar flares significantly affect planet X?
- Did gravitational encounters with other planets reshape its orbit?

---

## 15. Mission-Specific Discoveries

These are discoveries tied to the success and design of specific missions.

- Did the probe confirm or refute the presence of an atmosphere on planet X?
- Did high-resolution imaging reveal unexpected surface features on planet X?
- Did radar mapping uncover subsurface structures (lava tubes, oceans, buried craters)?
- Did landers successfully obtain and analyze surface samples from planet X?
- Did magnetometer readings confirm or disprove the existence of a magnetic field?
- Did gravity measurements refine the internal structure model of planet X?
- Did repeated observations reveal temporal changes (seasons, storms, surface changes)?
- Did follow-up missions overturn earlier interpretations about planet X?

---

## 16. System-Level Synthesis Discoveries

These are big-picture conclusions combining many lines of data.

- How did this entire planetary system likely form?
- How did the system’s architecture evolve (migration, scattering, resonances)?
- Why does this system differ from typical systems (e.g. many super-Earths, hot Jupiter, etc.)?
- How common might systems like this be in the galaxy?
- Which planets in this system were or are potentially habitable?
- Did life arise in this system at all, and if so, where and when?
- How has the presence or absence of giant planets influenced the inner system?
- Is this system dynamically young, old, or still evolving in a significant way?
